How to Get a Free GPU for AI Training 💀

Published on: March 8, 2025 | Category: AI, Free GPU

🔥 The GPU Crisis: Why This Guide Exists

GPUs are expensive as hell. AI devs are out here selling kidneys for RTX 4090s, while we’re out here training models for FREE. If you want a Tesla T4, A100, or even an H100 without spending a dime, this guide is for you.

🚀 1. Google Colab – The Free Pain-in-the-Ass GPU

Google was feeling generous and gave us Tesla T4s for free. But then they said, "nah, pay up," and started disconnecting us mid-training.

What You Get: Free Tesla T4 for a few hours
Downside: Random disconnects, session limits, and Google watching your code.

💡 How to Keep Colab Alive Longer?


# Stops Colab from disconnecting (sometimes works)
from google.colab import output
output.eval_js('new Promise(resolve => {
    setInterval(() => {
        document.querySelector("colab-toolbar-button").click()
    }, 60000)
})')

🔥 2. Kaggle – Colab's Smarter Twin Brother

Google owns Kaggle, and unlike Colab, they let you train models properly. No random disconnects, longer sessions, and faster storage.

💡 How to Get an A100?

1️⃣ Start a new Kaggle Notebook
2️⃣ Go to Settings > Accelerator
3️⃣ Pray to the GPU gods that you get an A100 💀

💰 3. Paperspace – Free Tesla V100 (If You’re Lucky)

Paperspace is like Colab on steroids. They sometimes give free GPUs, and if you catch them, you’re golden.

What You Get: Tesla T4 / V100
Downside: Waitlist for free GPUs, needs a sign-up

👀 4. The Cloud Gaming Abuse Method 💀

Ok, this one's cursed. Cloud gaming platforms like GeForce NOW, Stadia (RIP), and Shadow PC have powerful GPUs for gaming. Some smart devs figured out how to run Python scripts inside these services. If you know, you know. 👀

📢 Final Thoughts

Moral of the story: If you’re broke but still wanna train AI models, there are ways to get free GPUs. Just don’t abuse it too hard.
